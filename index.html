The Project

The project brief was to redesign a clients website, and as the Lead of the group, the main ideas were to restructure the content, layout and add interactive features to the website. Also we were to get a video team to record an interview with the client with which we were to use in his redesigned website.

Deliverables

  • implementing 360 Degrees image viewer with a slider, hover state or automatic.
  • Scroll back to top - Wordpress plugin
  • Optimizing the website for SEO.
  • Video Interview

Gathering information and setting meetings with client

We started researching and determining subsequent steps we are going to take. The most important task was to get a clear understanding of the purpose of the project, the main goals and what the client want to achieve, and also we considered the target audience the client wants to attract to his site. Meetings were set-up with the client to get as much information from him as posible.

Planning: Sitemap and Wireframe Creation

At this stage of the developmental cycle, we created data that can give to a customer an opportunity to judge how the entire site will look like. On the basis of the information that we gathered together in the previous phase, we created the sitemap which gave us an understanding of how the final product would look like.it also showed us the relationship between different pages on the website and how they may be connected. The sitemap described the relations between the main areas of the website. , so you can judge how easy it will be for the end-user to find the required information or service if he starts from the main page. The main reason behind the sitemap creation was to build a user-friendly and easy to navigate website. The sitemap allowed us to understand how the inner structure of the website would look like, but doesn’t describe the users interface. In order to to get approval from the client, we created a mock-up and a wireframe which is a visual representation of user interface that we were going to create. The mock-ups only described the elements that will be added to the page and their location.

Design: Page Layouts, Review, and Approval Cycle

During the design phase, all the visual content, such as images, photos, and videos were created . Once again, all the information that we gathered from the first phase is very crucial. That is; feedback from the client, the customer and target audience must be considering during the design process.

Content Writing and Assembly

At this step we put in writing the very essence we wanted to communicate to the audience of the website, and added calls-to-action. we used all the text from the clients previous website as he wanted to maintain them, which was better for us to compile all the text content before we started with the coding phase.

Coding

At this step, we started with creating the website . Graphic elements that have been designed during the previous stages were used to create an actual website. Usually, we started off with the home page, and then all sub-pages are added, according to the website hierarchy that was previously created in the form of a sitemap. Frameworks and CMS should be implemented to make sure that server can handle the installation and set-up smoothly. All the static web page elements that were designed during the mock-up and layout stage were created and tested. Then, special features and interactivity were also added.

Testing, Review and Launch

We tested every link to make sure that there are no broken ones among them. And also checked every script, run a spell-checking software to find possible typos. And used code validators to check if the code follows the current web standards. Valid code is necessary, for example, if cross-browser compatibility is important for you. After we checked and re-check your website, we then uploaded the website to the server using an FTP (File Transfer Protocol) software in this case we used FileZilla. After we deployed the files, we run yet another, final test just to be sure that all your files have been installed correctly.

Maintenance: Opinion Monitoring and Regular Updating

What’s important to remember is that a website is more a service than a product. It’s not enough to “deliver” a website to a user. so we made sure that everything worked fine, and that the client was satisfied with the project.


UXD Project - 2018

Catering services Uclan (University of Central Lancashire).
UXD methods and solutions generatons.

Creating and implementing a questionnaire for users and non users of the Catering services provided in Harrington and Foster building at the University of Central Lancashire.


The project explored a variety of UX techniques which were used to improved the digital product and service of the UCLan – Catering Services,and these methods were used to redesign user experience (UX).

Methods explored:

  • Questionnaires
  • Focus Groups
  • Surveys
  • Interviews

Analysing Methods:

The method assigned to me was focus group, which I did with another team member, We carefully planned and designed questions that were meant to probe the participants into giving their opinions and feelings. We focused on groups around the media café and Harrington Café, timing was very important as most student finish most of their lecturers about 3pm, so we started of about 3pm when students were either finished with lecturers of going to get a bite to eat. We interviewed about three different groups of five, four and three participants. Our first participant who were in a group of four, were organised homogeneously, in terms of similar age group, course of study, either part time or full time and people usually already in groups, this contributed to us having a much comfortable environment and helped participants to freely speak. The interviews yield both qualitative and observational data, which were video recorded with the participant’s permissions. However, I found out that most participants tend to agree with each other which caused a bit of a problem for us, As i felt the data collected was somewhat not as we had hope for. After all the methods had been explored we decided to use the Questionnaire method as that seemed to have collected relevant data that was useful to our project. It was an experience which if I was to do again I would arranged to interview more than just four or five participants but rather eight and perhaps arrange a room with table and chairs to make it much comfortable for both us the moderators and the participants.

Analysing the results

The focus group results were in-depth as oppose to the questionnaire and survey methods, as the participants were able to share their judgments and opinions rather than just a YES or No answer. The focus group interview brought out issues of great interest between the participants, which we were able to record, and later transferred the data onto paper for our records. The Data collected from the Focus groups were very difficult to analysis as it were more based on feeling and opinions as opposed to data collected from the survey, which provided a much quantifiable data, which we could easily analyse. Furthermore, with the questionnaire method, there was less time involved in collecting the data and the cost of sampling respondent was quite lower however, there was no ability to probe respondents in regards to their responses as oppose to the other methods we explored. With the Interview method, the data collected were accurate in terms of false responses; as the participant could not, for example give false information about sex (just an example since we did maintain gender neutrality through out our research methods) or whether or not he or she was a student on campus. As much as interviews are adaptable, our team member who was assigned the interview method faced some difficulties, as most participants were not interested in sparing a few minutes for an interview.

Changes made

Using Google forms, Posters, and QR code which would be scanned by the user with a mobile device and then he/she could fill in the questionnaires on their devices, as most of the potential users are students, seemed much better and up to date than the old paper questionnaire previously used by Uclan eat. The client seemed much please and from my point of view it would be easier and much convenient for the potential customers as well. Overall our project went very well and I have learnt a lot from working in a team to real live client and to producing a system usable by a business.

Process

  • Wireframes
  • Personas - based on findings.
  • Style-Guide - from Uclan Eat.
  • Final Product using Google Forms.
